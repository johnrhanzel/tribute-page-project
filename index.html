Dr. Norman Borlaug

He is known as the Father of Green Revolution

More information about Dr. Norman Borlaugh:

  • Born on March 25, 1914 from Saude, Iowa, U.S
  • Died on September 12, 2009 from Dallas, Texas
  • He is an American agricultural scientist, and plant pathologist
  • He won the Nobel Prize for Peace in 1970
  • He laid the groundwork for agricultural technology, which has helped to lessen world hunger
  • Borlaug earned a Ph.D. in plant pathology from the University of Minnesota after studying plant biology and forestry there
  • Borlaug experimented with novel wheat varieties, creating disease-resistant strains that could withstand the harsh climate, in order to assist impoverished farmers who struggled with diseased and low-producing crops
